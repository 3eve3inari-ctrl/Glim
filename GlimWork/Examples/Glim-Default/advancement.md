# Advancement Criteria — Glim Default Campaign

Standard Glim advancement rules apply (see `references/rules.md`). The triggers below are tuned for this campaign's themes.

## When to Award Advancement

The PC may earn advancement when at least one of the following is true at session end:

### Mechanical Triggers
- **Used a proficiency under real pressure** — a Trained skill applied in a scene where failure had consequences. Repeated, demonstrated competence may upgrade Trained → Expert.
- **Survived a serious injury or stress 4-5 event.** The PC has changed in some way they can articulate.
- **Spent all 3 Story Points in a session** on meaningful interventions (not just bonuses).

### Setting-Specific Triggers
- **Genuine contact with a Vahn line** — first conversation with a named Vahn individual; first invitation onto Vahn ground; first time a Vahn extends a courtesy that suggests they remember the PC.
- **A successful Glim breakthrough** — the first time the PC sees the Glim do something they did not expect, *and* notes it (not just experienced it). Possible new proficiency: Glim Theory, Glim Siphoning, etc.
- **Survived a dungeon descent.** The PC has been *in* a Glim-thick place where the rules do not hold and came back. Possible new proficiency: Wayfinding, Composure under Wrongness, or a campaign-specific resilience.
- **Exposed to a Glim-quirk threshold.** If the PC has used the Glim repeatedly, an emergent quirk should manifest (light sensitivity, certain dreams, an inability to lie under open sky). This is a narrative beat, not a stat increase, but it is part of growth.

## Discussion Prompts

When discussing advancement at session end, ask:
- *What did the character learn this session that they did not know before?*
- *What about how they see the world — the Hold, the Vahn, the Glim — has shifted?*
- *Did they discover a new edge in themselves, or a new limit?*

The advancement should reflect the answer.

## Story Point Reset

Story Points reset to 3 on advancement. They do *not* reset between sessions otherwise.

## Notes

- The campaign's central question is *who does the PC become in this place* — keep advancement tied to that. A purely mechanical level-up without a narrative shift is a missed beat.
- Avoid awarding advancement for combat alone unless the combat involved real choice (who to spare, what to risk). Killing a monster is not, by itself, growth.
