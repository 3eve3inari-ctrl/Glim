# Lantern Hold

## Description
A wooden palisade six months old, raised on a high meadow that backs into a tree line of old pine. The walls are uneven — built fast, repaired faster — and stand twice the height of a tall settler. Inside: a packed-earth common, four longhouses (two for sleeping, one for stores, one half-finished workshop), and a watchtower at the meadow's eastern lip. At night the place is *lit* — pitch-soaked lanterns hang every four paces along the walls. The Glim is unusually bright here, and the lanterns are how the watch sees what the Glim reveals before it reaches the gate.

## Key Features
- **The watchtower** — eastern wall, three stories, manned at all hours. Best vantage on the woods.
- **The naturalist's workshop** — half-built shed in the southwest corner. Glass jars, copper coils, charcoal sketches pinned to the walls. Kenric Maul lives and works here.
- **The well** — stone-lined, unusually cold. Some say the Glim is densest in the water. Drawing it requires two hands and a steady stance.
- **The bear-stone** — a flat, lichen-covered stone in the meadow's western quarter, half-buried. Predates the palisade. Settlers use it as a bench. The Vahn used it for something else.
- **The chapel** — newest structure, simple wood frame, no roof yet. Chaplain Ennis insists on completing it before winter.
- **The gate** — single door, oak banded with iron, opens inward. Closed at sundown.

## Who's Here

**Population:** ~60 settlers. Mix of laborers, two stonemasons, a smith (Mor Halek), Captain Daven and her three sworn officers, the naturalist Kenric Maul, the chaplain Ennis, a healer apprentice named Sarith, and a rotating watch.

## History
- **Six months ago:** Charter party landed at the river mouth, marched inland 11 days, and chose the meadow for its high ground and unobstructed sightlines.
- **Three months ago:** First monster attack at the wall. One settler dead. The walls were heightened the next week.
- **Six weeks ago:** Captain Daven's second meeting with a bear-line emissary ended with both parties leaving without speaking. Whatever the emissary came to say went unsaid. No bear-line Vahn has been seen at the wall since.
- **Last week:** A settler scout went out two days east and returned wrong — couldn't say what they'd seen, couldn't sleep, left through the gate the next night without his rifle. Has not returned.

## Current Status
The Hold is *just* working. Walls hold; food is rationed but adequate; deaths are slow but real. Tension inside the palisade is rising — not panic, but a careful, watchful quiet. The captain's authority is unquestioned, but her decisions are increasingly second-guessed in private. The naturalist's research is starting to suggest things about the Glim that may not be welcome news.

---

*The Hold is an instrument, not a home. It exists to claim the meadow for the crown. Whether it can survive the claim is the open question.*
