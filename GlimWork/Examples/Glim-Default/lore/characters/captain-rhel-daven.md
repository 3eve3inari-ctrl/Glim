# Captain Rhel Daven

## Form
A woman in her early forties, weathered the way driftwood is weathered — pale where the sun never touches and dark where it does. Tall, narrow, walks like she's measuring the ground. Hair cropped short and gray-blond, kept under a pinned cap when she's outside. Wears charter-issue leather and a long coat that has been mended in seven visible places. A flintlock at her hip; a knife on the opposite side; a small silver pin at her collar marking her commission.

## Abilities
- **Charter authority** — she can lawfully bind the expedition to agreements, requisition labor, and execute discipline. The settlers know this.
- **Combat-competent** — Expert with the flintlock and Trained with a long knife. Not a duelist, but reliably lethal at her ranges.
- **Reads people** — twenty years of running parties of strangers in conditions that wear them down. She knows when someone is about to break, lie, or run.
- **Knows the charter terms cold** — what the crown will and will not back her on. This is her primary protection.

## Personality
- **Hard but not cruel.** She has buried more people than most, including thirty-five from her last command. She does not get casual about it. She also does not let it stop her from giving an order that may add to the count.
- **Believes in the charter as a contract, not a flag.** The crown will abandon them if they fail; the charter is what survives. This shapes every decision she makes about settler-Vahn contact.
- **Speaks short.** Long sentences only when she's tired or has to explain something for the second time. When she does talk at length, listen.
- **Will not lie to her people.** She will withhold, defer, or refuse to answer. She will not say a thing she knows to be false.
- **Distrusts the Glim.** Has seen what self-taught practice does to a person. Tolerates Kenric because she has to, and because she respects him.

## History
- **First expedition (twelve years ago):** Charter to the southern reach. Foothold lasted four years. Returned with seven of forty-two. The crown's debrief was brief. She was given another commission within a year because she came back at all.
- **Years between:** Trained junior officers in the homeland. Refused two postings because the locations were "set up to fail." Took the Lantern Hold charter because the meadow had high ground and the river was navigable.
- **The bear-line meetings:** Two formal exchanges, three months apart. The first went politely and produced nothing. The second ended in silence on both sides. She has not asked for a third.
- **The scout who left:** A settler named Bren who walked out the gate at night last week. She knew him from her last expedition. She has not spoken his name since.

## Current Status
**Relationship to PC:** Commanding officer. Will assess the PC's competence in the first scene; her opinion of them will shape what missions they get and how much rope they're given. She does not extend trust quickly.

**Where she is:** Inside the Hold most days. Walks the wall every morning. Visits the watchtower at sunset. Sleeps in the smaller longhouse with her three sworn officers.

**What she wants right now:** To know what is in the eastern woods that took Bren. To get through the winter without losing the Hold. To find a way to talk to the bear-line that does not require her to apologize for things the crown will not let her apologize for.

---

*Rhel is the gravity well of the early game. The PC will orbit her whether they want to or not — she is who they report to, and her judgments about what is and isn't allowed will be the friction that defines their first arc.*
