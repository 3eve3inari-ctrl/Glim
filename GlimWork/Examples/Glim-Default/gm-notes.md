## What's Actually in the Eastern Woods

The scout Bren who walked out the gate at night last week is alive and is currently traveling with a wolf-line Vahn named Aelis. Bren did not desert in the conventional sense — he experienced something on a ranging that he could not contain inside the Hold's framework, and Aelis found him before exposure or panic killed him. Bren is being kept alive in a hunting camp two days east. He is not a prisoner. He is also not free to leave, in the sense that the Vahn who found him will not let him return to the Hold while he is still in the state he is in.

**What he saw:** A *Waking* in progress. Not a dungeon mouth opening — something rarer. A glade where the Glim was so thick it was visible by daylight, and a creature was forming inside it — not yet fully real, not yet fully one thing. A bear-line elder named Esha was at the glade, singing the thing into a shape it could survive in. Bren did not understand any of this. He saw what he thought was a monster being born and a Vahn doing magic on it. The combination broke something in his ability to frame what was happening.

**Implications:**
- If the PC tracks Bren east, they will eventually meet Aelis (wolf-line, hostile-cautious) and possibly Esha (bear-line elder, weary-patient).
- The Wakings are how the Vahn understand the appearance of monsters. They can sometimes intervene — singing the unbound thing into a livable form. They cannot do this for every Waking; they have not been trying for many years because the settlers' presence makes the rituals more dangerous (more witnesses, more interference).
- This is the seed of the campaign's first arc: the PC may discover that the monsters the settlers fight are the failed Wakings — the ones the Vahn could not reach in time, often because of settler activity.

## Captain Daven's Actual Position

Rhel is closer to despair than she lets show. The bear-line emissary at the second meeting (six weeks ago) said, in halting trade-tongue: "Leave the meadow before the cold comes. We will not ask a third time." She has not told her officers this. She has not decided what to do.

She is also harboring a private plan: if the Hold cannot survive the winter, she intends to fall back to the river mouth and overwinter there in tents, then send a messenger ship for reinforcements in the spring. The crown will see this as failure. She will lose her commission. She is prepared for this if it saves the settlers.

If the PC earns her trust, she will eventually share some of this. She will not share all of it.

## Kenric Maul's Research

Kenric has been quietly mapping Glim density across the meadow and has noticed that the brightness pattern matches no natural feature he knows — it follows a rough oval centered slightly *east* of the bear-stone in the western quarter. He has not yet hypothesized why. The answer is that the bear-stone is one of several anchor stones the bear-line uses for ritual; the meadow is a Glim-rich site by Vahn intent, not by accident. Kenric will figure this out within the next two sessions if undisturbed.

When he figures it out, he will not immediately tell Captain Daven. He will tell Toren first.

## Bear-Line Stance (Internal)

The bear-line is preparing a *third* meeting that they will not announce. Esha, the elder currently at the eastern Waking, intends to bring Bren back to the wall when he is stable, and use his return as the basis for one final attempt to talk. This will happen in 6-10 in-game days, depending on Bren's recovery.

If the PC encounters Esha first, the timeline shifts; the third meeting may happen sooner, or may be replaced by a private exchange between Esha and the PC.
