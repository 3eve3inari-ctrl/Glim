# Practical Reference

Scratch.md tips and in-session patterns that apply across campaigns.

---

## What to Track

**Rule of thumb:** If it would hurt to lose it, track it.

Track automatically when the PC:
- Spends resources (money, ammo, fuel, favors)
- Uses a limited consumable
- Takes on obligations (debts, promises, contracts)
- Acquires something significant

**Don't track:**
- Trivial expenses that don't affect the fiction
- Resources that are assumed plentiful (basic meals in a city, common ammo if well-supplied)

When uncertain, ask: "Would the character notice this leaving their pocket?" If yes, log it.

---

## Ledger Format

Keep entries simple and timestamped by reason:

```
**[Resource]:**
- +X (source/reason)
- -X (spent on)
```

Examples across genres:
- `+50 credits (fence job)` / `-20 credits (bribe, dock guard)`
- `+3 gold (claviger nest)` / `-2 gold (room and board)`
- `ammo: -1 mag (firefight at warehouse)`
- `favors: +1 (saved Renn's kid)` / `-1 (called in with Syndicate)`

---

## NPC Tracking

When a new NPC appears, note immediately:

```
**[Name]** — one-line description (dossier type)
```

Dossier types:
- `full` — Has motivations, agency, ongoing threads
- `background` — Named character who might reappear

Examples:
- `**Sorrel** — veteran crew leader, runs tight ship (full)`
- `**Tessler** — dwarf gem dealer, doesn't ask names (background)`

Also track what NPCs *in the current scene* know — prevents knowledge bleed.

---

## Consumables Format

When the party acquires a limited-use item:

```
**[Item Name]** (X uses)
Effect: [What it does mechanically]
Trigger: [When/how to use it]
```

Decrement uses in scratch as spent; reconcile at session end.

## Party Combat

When the PC fights alongside NPC allies:

**Simple (allies as circumstance):**
- Allied NPCs grant +2 to PC's combat rolls
- Narrate their contributions as part of PC's success/failure
- If PC fails badly, an ally might take the hit instead (GM choice)

**Complex (allies act independently):**
- Roll for each NPC using tier modifiers (Competent +3, Expert +5, Master +7)
- They have their own actions, their own risks
- Use when ally actions matter narratively

**When to use which:**
- Quick skirmish, allies are backdrop → Simple
- Dramatic fight, ally might die or shine → Complex
- When in doubt → Simple (faster, keeps focus on PC)

## Emergent PC Abilities

When the PC develops a new capability through play (not advancement), describe it narratively first, then decide if it needs mechanical backing.

**Tiers of formalization:**
1. **Narrative only** — No mechanical effect, just flavor
2. **Situational bonus** — Grants advantage or +2 in specific circumstances
3. **New proficiency** — If broadly applicable, consider at next advancement
4. **Special ability** — If unique and powerful, give it a cost (stress, SP, or limitation)

**When to formalize:** If you've referenced the ability 3+ times and it's affecting play, give it mechanical definition. Until then, narrative is fine.
